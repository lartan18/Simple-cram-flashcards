List the three core pillars of the CIA triad and briefly explain each.\Confidentiality* involves keeping data secret from unauthorized parties; Integrity ensures data is not corrupt (Data) or hijacked (Control); Availability ensures systems are accessible with good response times and storage.$
Explain the difference between Privacy and Confidentiality according to the course material.\Privacy* is the right to be left alone and protects personal information, whereas *Confidentiality* focuses on protecting business secrets and sensitive communication.$
How does Accountability differ from Non-Repudiation?\Accountability* uses logs and audit trails to track who did what, while Non-Repudiation prevents a party from denying an interaction, often using digital signatures.$
Identify three common "weak links" in information security.\Common weak links include **weak passwords**, human errors (social engineering), and **poor software** implementation.$
Explain the principle of "Defense in Depth" and provide three examples of layers.\Defense in Depth avoids reliance on a single security measure; examples include using a firewall, authentication, and cryptography in combination.$
What is the "Swiss Cheese Model" in the context of security?\It is a visual metaphor where each security layer has "holes" (vulnerabilities), but the solid parts of the next layer cover those holes to block an attack path.$
Explain the principle of "Fail Securely" with a real-world example.\It means systems should maintain protection if a control fails; for example, a firewall should block all traffic if it crashes rather than allowing everything through.$
What are the two dimensions of the "Principle of Least Privilege"?\The two dimensions are **minimal access** (only the permissions needed for the job) and **minimal time** (automatic logout after inactivity).$
Explain why "Security by Obscurity" is considered an insufficient principle.\Hiding secrets is hard because attackers often find weaknesses; while it may be necessary, it is not enough to secure a system on its own.$
List four specific types of attacks/threats mentioned in Lecture 1.\Examples include **web defacement**, **phishing**, **ransomware**, and **Denial of Service (DoS)**.$
